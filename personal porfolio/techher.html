Challenges and How I Overcame Them

üß†

Imposter Syndrome

The Challenge: Constantly questioning whether I truly belonged in tech, especially when I was the only woman in advanced algorithms class.

How I Overcame It:

  • Kept a "wins journal" documenting every achievement, no matter how small
  • Found a mentor who reminded me that everyone feels like an imposter sometimes
  • Focused on continuous learning rather than comparing myself to others
‚öñÔ∏è

Unconscious Bias

The Challenge: Dealing with assumptions about my technical abilities and being interrupted or talked over in meetings.

How I Overcame It:

  • Prepared extensively for meetings and came with data to back up my points
  • Found allies who would amplify my voice and give me credit for my ideas
  • Learned to confidently interrupt back and claim my space in conversations
üë•

Lack of Representation

The Challenge: Having few female role models in leadership positions and feeling isolated in my career journey.

How I Overcame It:

  • Actively sought out women in tech communities and online networks
  • Attended women-focused tech events and joined mentorship programs
  • Became the representation I wished I had by mentoring younger women

Women Who Inspire Me

These incredible women have paved the way and continue to inspire my journey in tech.

Dr. Fei-Fei Li

Co-Director of Stanford Human-Centered AI Institute

A pioneer in computer vision and AI, Dr. Li's work on ImageNet revolutionized machine learning. She advocates for AI that serves humanity and has been instrumental in promoting diversity in AI research.

Key Achievement: Created ImageNet, the dataset that sparked the deep learning revolution

Reshma Saujani

Founder & CEO of Girls Who Code

A lawyer turned tech advocate, Reshma founded Girls Who Code to close the gender gap in technology. Her organization has reached over 450,000 girls worldwide, inspiring the next generation of female technologists.

Key Achievement: Built the largest pipeline of future female engineers in the US

Susan Wojcicki

Former CEO of YouTube

One of Google's earliest employees, Susan led YouTube to become the world's largest video platform. She championed creator economy and consistently advocated for women's advancement in tech leadership roles.

Key Achievement: Grew YouTube from startup to 2+ billion monthly users

What I've Learned Along the Way

01

Your Voice Matters

Don't wait for permission to speak up. Your unique perspective brings value that others might miss.

02

Find Your Tribe

Seek out communities of women in tech. Having allies and mentors makes the journey less lonely.

03

Embrace Failure

Every bug, failed project, or rejection is a learning opportunity. Resilience is your superpower.

04

Lift Others Up

As you climb, extend a hand to pull others up with you. Representation creates more representation.

05

Stay Authentic

Don't try to fit into someone else's mold. The tech world needs your authentic self and unique approach.
